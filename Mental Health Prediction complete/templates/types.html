Depression

A mood disorder that makes a person feel very sad, empty, or hopeless for a long time. It's more than just feeling sad for a day or two; it lasts for weeks or even months. People with depression might lose interest in activities they used to enjoy, have trouble sleeping, or feel very tired all the time. They might also have difficulty concentrating, eating too much or too little, and feeling worthless or guilty. Depression can affect anyone, and itâ€™s not a sign of weakness.

Anxiety

Anxiety is a feeling of worry or fear about what might happen. It's like your mind is always on alert, expecting something bad. Sometimes, you might feel nervous for no clear reason. Your heart may beat fast, your hands might sweat, or you could feel dizzy. It's normal to feel anxious before a test or a big event, but for some people, these feelings happen too often and can make everyday life hard. This is called an anxiety disorder.

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder is a mental health condition that causes extreme mood swings. These mood swings include emotional highs, known as mania or hypomania, and lows, called depression.

During a manic phase, a person may feel very happy, energetic, or overly confident. They might take risks or make impulsive decisions.

During a depressive phase, the same person can feel very sad, hopeless, and lose interest in activities they once enjoyed. These mood changes can affect sleep, energy levels, behavior, and the ability to think clearly.

Autism

Autism, or Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), is a condition that affects how a person thinks, learns, and interacts with others. People with autism often have challenges with social skills, communication, and repetitive behaviors. They might find it hard to understand social cues, such as body language or tone of voice, making it difficult to make friends or understand others' feelings.

Every person with autism is different. Some might have strong skills in areas like math, art, or music, while others may need more help with daily tasks. Autism can be mild or severe, and people with autism may need different levels of support.

Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia is a mental illness that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. People with schizophrenia may have trouble knowing what is real and what is not. They might see or hear things that others do not (hallucinations) or believe things that are not true (delusions). Their thoughts can be very disorganized, making it hard to talk or concentrate. Schizophrenia can also cause a lack of motivation and difficulty in expressing emotions.
